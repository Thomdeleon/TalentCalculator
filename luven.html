Luven Beourgest

Thornmail (2nd Level Transmutation)

2nd Level Transmutation spell. Requires Concentration. As an action, you can grant an ally within 30 feet the thornmail property for 1 minute. Thornmail armor reflects 50% of any melee attack damage, or 25% of any ranged attack damage, back as piercing damage.
  • Armory of Thorns: Costs 1 seed of life. Target an additional ally with this spell
  • Self-Sustaining: Costs 3 seeds of life. Removes Concentration from the spell.
  • Circle of Life: Costs 2 seed of life. Regain the spell slot used to cast this spell.

Touching Grass

You gain the following benefits while standing on natural ground or while under open sky:
  • Perspective: As an action, you can end one ongoing charmed, frightened, or other mind-altering effect on yourself.
  • Focus: You have advantage on all concentration checks, as well as on any intelligence, wisdom, or charisma saving throw.
  • Meditation: Costs 1 Seed of Life. Requires concentration. As a bonus action, you may start meditating, gaining 1d8+wisdom modifier hp each round for up to 1 minute.
  • Tranquility: Costs 4 Seeds of Life. Requires concentration. As a bonus action, you grant Perspective, Focus, and Meditation to all allies within 60 feet for 1 minute.

Wonderberry Bush (4th Level Transmutation)

4th level Transmutation Spell. Casting time 1 minute. Requires ground that can support plant life. You magically grow a Wonderberry Bush full of 4d12+12 berries. For each 1d12+3 berries, you may choose one of the four following berry types. Up to 12 berries can be consumed at one time in the same way one would drink a potion. Harvested berries expire after 24 hours. Harvesting a full bush requires a minute of work. Unharvested berries expire after 3 days. Wonderberry Bushes regrow their berries after 1 year. All of the listed berries heal for 1d4+0, in addition to their listed affects.
  • Butterberry: Tastes incredible. Improves the quality of any dishes made with it.
  • Miracle Berry: Cures the poisoned, blinded, or deafened condition. Makes sour things taste sweet.
  • Cloudberry: Makes you feel light as a cloud. Grants Gaseous Form for 1 minute.
  • Meadberry: You have advantage on all concentration checks, as well as on any intelligence, wisdom, or charisma saving throw.
  • Accelerated Growth: Costs 1 Seed of Life. Casting time of Wonderberry Bush is reduced to 1 action.
  • Luscious Berries: Costs 2 Seeds of Life. All berries heal for 1d6+0 instead.
  • Circle of Life: Costs 4 Seeds of Life. Regain your 4th level spell slot after casting Wonderberry Bush.

Arm of Thresh

You may turn your arm into a series of twisting, living roots with a 30 foot range. You gain the following:
  • Improved Shillelagh: Your arm is treated as a Shillelagh with a 30 foot range. As an action, you may make a 30 foot ranged attack, dealing damage equal to 5d8 + wisdom at level 21. This increases by 1d8 at level 29 to a maximum of 6d8 + wisdom.
  • Root Bind: As a action, you may attempt to restrain a target. Use your wisdom score in place of strength for this check.
  • Lifegrip: Costs 1 Seed of Life. As an action, you may pull any ally your size or less within 30 feet towards you. You may also pull yourself to a fixed object with this ability. This movement does not provoke attacks of opportunity.
  • Anchoring Root: Costs 2 Seeds of Life. Doubles the range of your Arm of Thresh to 60 feet and grants advantage on Root Bind and Improved Shillelagh for 1 minute.

Regrowth

Your next healing spell also heals over time, restoring an amount equal to the minimum dice roll at the start of every turn for 0 turns. If the healing is a fixed amount (such as with the spell Heal), it heals 1/5th of its normal amount every turn. You may apply regrowth to any healing spell once per short rest. The Regrowth effect ends when the target drops to zero hit points or whenever the duration ends.
  • Lifebloom: Costs 1 Seed of Life. As a bonus action, the target gains all of its remaining healing over time instantly.
  • Regenerate: Costs 5 Seeds of Life. Your regrowth ability now no longer ends when the target drops to 0 hp. Upon dropping to 0 health, a target with regrowth must still make a death save, but is instantly brought back above 0 hp thereafter.
  • Circle of Life: Costs 2 Seeds of Life. You may cast regrowth again. The regrowth effect does not stack. The one with the highest healing or longest duration takes precedence.

Tree of Shelter (5th level Transmutation)

As an action, you can magically grow a Tree of Shelter with a 25 foot radius for 1 minute. It has the following traits:
  • Shelter: Creatures under the Tree of Shelter have advantage on dexterity saving throws and have the benefits of Evasion while under its branches.
  • Lashing: Any hostile creature you designate that steps within 5 feet of the tree gets attacked for 4d6 bludgeoning damage and must roll a dexterity saving throw against your spell DC to avoid becoming restrained on hit. A creature that starts its turn in the area and is already restrained by the branches takes 4d6 bludgeoning damage. A creature restrained by the branches can use its action to make a Strength or Dexterity check (its choice) against your spell save DC. On a success, it frees itself.
  • Awaken: Costs 3 Seeds of Life. Removes concentration from Tree of Shelter.
  • Circle of Life: Costs 3 Seeds of Life. Regain the spell slot used to summon the Tree of Shelter.

Swiftmending

You can cast any healing or restoration spell that takes one action or bonus action to cast as a reaction. You can perform swiftmending once per day.
  • Nature's Swiftness: Costs 1 Seed of Life. You can cast any spell that takes one action or bonus action as a reaction, not just a healing or restoration spell.
  • Regenerate: Costs 2 Seeds of Life. You may cast any healing or restoration spell that takes 1 action as a bonus action.
  • Circle of Life: Costs 2 Seeds of Life. You may use swiftmending again.

Tree of Life

You can use your Wild Shape ability to change into a medium-sized treant. You gain the following traits while in Tree of Life form:
  • Tree of Life: The potency of your healing spells increases by 50% while in this form.
  • Natural Healer: You may cast multiple healing or restoration spells per turn while in this form.
  • Ponderous: Your movement speed is reduced by 10 feet.
  • Nature's Reach: The range of your spells is doubled. If the range is touch, the range increases to 30 feet.
  • Barkskin: You are under the effects of barkskin without the need for concentration.
  • Ironwood: Costs 2 Seeds of Life. As a bonus action, you may gain +2 AC for 1 minute. You can decide to cast Ironwood as part of your Wild Shape action to turn into a Tree of Life.

Black Lotus (9th level Conjuration)

9th Level Conjuration Spell. You summon the black lotus as a bonus action, lasting for 1 minute. While holding the black lotus, you may use each of its three effects. Using an effect removes that portion of the black lotus until nothing remains.
  • Detachment (Stem): As an action, you remove all negative and positive conditions from a target creature within 60 feet.
  • Rebirth (Pistil): As an action, restore a creature within touch range to full health or restore a creature who has died in the past day back to life with 1/2 health. If the one who summons the black lotus dies, they are automatically resurrected with half health on their next turn by this effect instead.
  • Enlightenment (Petals): Your next single target healing or restoration spell affects 0 creatures you designate within a 60 foot range.
  • Circle of Life: Costs 9 Seeds of Life. You regain the spell slot used to summon the Black Lotus.

Wild Growth

You gain the following benefits:
  • Genesis: The maximum amount of Seeds of Life you can carry doubles, increasing by 0.
  • Spontaneous Growth: Whenever you roll for initiative, roll a d4. You gain that many Seeds of Life.
  • Overgrowth: You gain 1 Seed of Life every time you roll maximum on a healing die roll, not just minimum.
  • Life Finds a Way: Costs 1 Seed of Life. You can reroll the result of any healing roll. You do not gain seeds for the results of any previous roll, only for the final result.
  • Miracle Growth: Costs up to 5 Seeds of Life. You may cast your next healing spell as if you were using a higher level spell slot, to a maximum of 9th level, without expending a higher level spell slot. Increase the level of your spell cast by 1 for every Seed of Life you spend in this manner, up to a maximum of 5.

    Magic Fang

    Your attacks while Wild Shaped may use your wisdom in place of your strength or dexterity for the purpose of attack and damage and your attacks while Wild Shaped are treated as magic for the purpose of overcoming damage reduction.

    Elemental Shape

    You can now use Wild Shape to transform into an air elemental, an earth elemental, a fire elemental, or a water elemental, so long as it does not exceed your maximum CR.

    Natural Shaper

    You may now Wild Shape as a bonus action instead of an action. Additionally, you can use your Wild Shape to alter your physical form in more subtle ways, as the spell Alter Self.

    Wild Flurry

    You may now attack an additional time when performing a multiattack or attack action while Wild Shaped.

    Trample

    While Wild Shaped as a creature that is at least one size category larger than another creature, you can pass through their areas without provoking attacks of opportunity. Additionally, you may use your reaction to make a single attack of opportunity against them if you are passing through their squares. If successful, the target must make an opposed strength check to avoid getting knocked prone.

    Maim

    Your attacks while Wild Shaped have additional effects based on their damage type:
    • Slashing: You may slash another creature within 5 feet of the original target.
    • Piercing: The target takes 1d4 additional bleed damage at the start of every turn until healed. This bleed damage stacks up to xd4. Any source of healing will stop the bleeding. Additionally, at the end of every turn, a creature can roll a constitution save against your spell DC to stop the bleeding.
    • Bludgeonging: If you are the same size as the target or larger, the target must make a strength save against your spell DC to avoid being knocked down or knocked back 5 feet.

    Berserk

    Your attacks while Wild Shaped have additional effects based on their damage type:
    • Frenzy: You are under the effects of Haste while Frenzied, doubling your movement speed and allowing you to make an additional single attack on your turn.
    • Survival Instincts: You are under the effects of Freedom of Movement while Frenzied.
    • Bloodthirsty: You have advantage on all attacks.
    • Dauntless: Your AC can no longer be less than 16, as if under the effects of Barkskin.
    • Iron Hide: You are under the effects of Freedom of Movement while Frenzied.
    • Trueshape: Your total hit points cannot be less than your base maximum HP while not Wild Shaped.
    • Regeneration: You gain 0 hp per round.
    • Rage: Your attacks deal additional damage equal to 0.
    • Feral Flurry: You may now attack an additional time when performing a multiattack or attack action while Wild Shaped. Stacks with Wild Flurry.
    • Pounce: You may make a single attack as a bonus action.

    Feral Instinct

    You now have two bonus actions and two reactions per round.

    Leader of the Pack

    Whenever you assume a new form through Wild Shape, you may grant your allies some of the passive traits of the creature you are Wild Shaped as. Select 0 number of traits from your Wild Shape's stat sheet. All allies use the selected traits of your Wild Shape instead of their own, unless they have a stronger version of that trait alraedy. Trait immunities granted through Leader of the Pack does not undo existing afflictions. For example, immunity to paralysis will not cure paralysis, it will just prevent subsequent attempts at paralysis.

Additionally, you can now mix parts of different creatures when wildshaped, so long as the total CR of the creatures mixed does not exceed your maximum CR. You gain the traits and abilities of all creatures you mix in this manner, choosing the higher of each creature's stats when they overlap.

    Leylines

    You can now sense where the leylines of the world are. Leylines are glowing 5 foot lines on the battlefield. Leylines rise far above the earth's surface, meaning most constructed places have a leyline. Standing on a leyline grants the following:
    • Nature's Reach: Doubles the range of your spells. Spells with a range of touch are turned into ranged spells with a range of 25+5x feet.
    • Weight of the World: Grants advantage on spell attacks and imposes disadvantage on saves against your spells.
    • Nature's Guidance: Your ranged spell attacks ignore half cover and three-quarters cover.
    • Channel Strength: The damage and healing of your spells is rolled with advantage.

    Innervate

    Once per short or long rest, you may cast Innervate on a friendly target within 30 feet as a bonus action. The target regains expended spell slots equal to 2 + 0 total spell levels.

    Spirit Totem

    You may summon one of the three following spirit totems as a bonus action. Upon summoning it and as a bonus action thereafter, you can move the totem up to 60 feet to a point you can see. It creates an aura in a 30-foot radius around it. It counts as neither a creature nor an object. The totem persists for 1 minute or until you're incapacitated. Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a short or long rest.
    • Bear Spirit: The bear spirit grants you and your allies its might and endurance. Each creature of your choice in the aura when the spirit appears gains temporary hit points equal to 25 + 0. In addition, you and your allies gain advantage on Strength checks and Strength saving throws while in the aura.
    • Hawk Spirit: The hawk spirit is a consummate hunter, aiding you and your allies with its keen sight. When a creature makes an attack roll against a target in the spirit's aura, you can use your reaction to grant advantage to that attack roll. In addition, you and your allies have advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks while in the aura.
    • Unicorn Spirit: The unicorn spirit lends its protection to those nearby. You and your allies gain advantage on all ability checks made to detect creatures in the spirit's aura. In addition, if you cast a spell using a spell slot that restores hit points to any creature inside or outside the aura, each creature of your choice in the aura also regains hit points equal to your druid level.

    Solid Fog (5th Level Conjuration)

    5th Level Conjuration spell. Requires concentration. This spell functions like Fog Cloud, but in addition to obscuring sight, the solid fog is so thick that any creature attempting to move through it progresses at a speed of 5 feet, regardless of its normal speed, and makes all melee attack rolls with disadvantage. The vapors prevent effective ranged weapon attacks (except for magic rays and the like). A creature or object that falls into solid fog is slowed, so that each 10 feet of vapor that it passes through reduces falling damage by 1d6. A creature can’t take a 5-foot step while in solid fog. However, unlike normal fog, only a severe wind (31+ mph) disperses these vapors, and it does so in 1 round.

    Duality

    You may now concentrate on two spells at once without penalty. If the spell requires a bonus action to use it on subsequent turns, you may use both concentration spells as a single bonus action. If you have the feat dual-focused, you can concentrate on a third spell, incurring the same penalities on the third spell as the feat specifies.

    Grounding Totem

    The next magical spell cast by creatures you designate into this area of effect is automatically counterspelled. For area of effect spells, if even an portion of the area of effect is within the Grounding Totem's radius, the entire spell is grounded. This expends the totem. Grounding totem always succeeds and cannot be counterspelled or dispelled. Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a short or long rest.
    You may summon a totem to your hand as a bonus action. Upon summoning it and as a bonus action thereafter, you can move the totem up to 60 feet to a point you can see. It creates an aura in a 30-foot radius around it. It counts as neither a creature nor an object. The totem persists for 1 minute or until you're incapacitated.

    Cyclone (9th Level Evocation)

    You may summon one of the three following spirit totems as a bonus action. Upon summoning it and as a bonus action thereafter, you can move the totem up to 60 feet to a point you can see. It creates an aura in a 30-foot radius around it. It counts as neither a creature nor an object. The totem persists for 1 minute or until you're incapacitated. Once you use this feature, you can't use it again until you finish a short or long rest.
    • If the cyclone passes through a creature's square, the creature must pass a dexterity save or it will take 8d6 bludgeoning damage initially and at the start of every turn it remains within the cyclone's radius.
    • If the creature fails its save and it is large or smaller, it is caught in the cyclone, being restrained and caught by it until the spell ends or until it somehow manages to teleport/anchor itself out of its effect. While restrained in this manner, a creature is carried in the cyclone, moving along with it as it crosses the battlefield. Teleporting out of a cyclone requires a spell check against your spell save DC.
    • For huge and gargantuan creatures, they must make a strength save against your spell DC or else they are restrained by this cyclone while it is on top of them. If they succeed, the cyclone is treated as difficult terrain.
    • A creature is also stunned the first time it is restrained by the cyclone and must roll a constitution saving throw at the end of every turn to stop being stunned.

    Gathering Storm

    You learn the wizard spells Lighting Bolt and Chain Lightning and always have them prepared, not counting against your list of prepared spells. Additionally, every time you cast a spell, you gain Gathering Storm stacks equal to the spell slot used, stacking to a maximum of 9. You may use these stacks to cast a Lightning Bolt or Chain Lightning without using up a spell slot, expending a number of stacks equal to the spell level used to cast it. Gathering Storm stacks expire when used or after 1 hour.

    Windfury Totem

    While windfury totem is active, all allies within 30 feet of it have a 25% chance of striking twice when making a melee attack.
    You may summon a totem to your hand as a bonus action. Upon summoning it and as a bonus action thereafter, you can move the totem up to 60 feet to a point you can see. It creates an aura in a 30-foot radius around it. It counts as neither a creature nor an object. The totem persists for 1 minute or until you're incapacitated.

    Spirit Guide

    Once per short or long rest, you may summon a spirit guide to your side. The spirit guide is incorporeal and cannot be targeted directly by effects while you are conscious (all such attacks are directed at you instead). This spirit guide has an action and a bonus action which it can use to cast any spell or cantrip you know on your turn, using up your spell slots. The spirit guide lasts for 1 minute and persists even while you are incapacitated or unconscious.
