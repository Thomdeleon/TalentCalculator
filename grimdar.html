Hostile Teleporter

Any teleportation spell which can be used on a willing creature can now also be used on a hostile creature too. The target must roll a wisdom save against your spell DC in order to avoid being teleported.

Object Teleportation (Cantrip)

As an action, you can teleport any object you are touching weighing less than 0 lbs up to 60 feet in any direction, so long as it is in line of sight and on solid ground. If this object is being held, the creature can make a wisdom saving throw to resist this effect.

Traveller

Your Far Step spell no longer requires concentration and now requires a movement action instead of a bonus action to use.

Transporter

You may bring along one willing creature whenever you cast a teleportation spell with a target of self. You must be touching the willing creature when casting the spell.

Redirect

0 times per day as a reaction, you can redirect an incoming ranged attack/ranged spell attack. Roll an opposed spell attack. If your roll beats the incoming attack roll, you are able to create a pair of portals that redirects the attack to whichever target you designate, using the attack roll you just rolled.

Trickster

You now have two bonus actions per turn and may cast multiple non-cantrip conjuration spells per turn.

Scatterbrain

You can now cast the scatter spell without using any spell slots 0 times per day. The number of creatures you can affect with a single scatter spell increases to 0.

Telefrag

You can now use teleportation spells to teleport creatures and objects into each other (if targeting multiple creatures, each one must fail their wisdom saving throw). This deals xd10 + 0x damage, where x is the spell level of the teleport cast (minimum 1). If used to teleport an explosive object into a target, the explosive deals critical damage and the target is unable to perform a dexterity saving throw to reduce or evade the damage of the explosive. Both targets end up next to each other after the spell ends, as if they were affected by a Teleport mishap.

Master Conjurer

0 times per day, you can designate the next conjuration spell to be cast as if using a 9th level spell slot. You still expend a spell slot equal to or higher than the lowest level spell slot required to cast it.

Send to the Sky

All of your conjuration spells which require the affected target(s) or portals to be placed on solid ground (such as Scatter, Arcane Portal, or Summon Elemental) no longer have this requirement, allowing you to place them anywhere within range. Your portal spells (i.e. Arcane Portal and Gate) can now be placed with any orientation.

Time Thief

While Slow is active on a target, you gain the effects of Haste. When Slow is active on multiple targets, you can buff an equivalent number of allies with the effects of Haste.

Stasis

0 times per day as an action, you can magically force a creature you can see within 60 feet of you to make a Constitution saving throw against your spell save DC. On a failed save the creature is incapacitated and has a speed of 0 until the end of your next turn or until the creature takes any damage.

Convergent Futures

You can peer through possible futures and magically pull one of them into events around you, ensuring a particular outcome. When you or a creature you can see within 60 feet of you makes an attack roll, an ability check, or a saving throw, you can use your reaction to ignore the die roll and decide whether the number rolled is the minimum needed to succeed or one less than that number (your choice). When you use this feature, you gain one level of exhaustion.

Time Dilation

(7th level Transmutation) Range 120 feet. Lasts for 1 minute. Requires concentration. You create a bubble of slowed time with a radius of 20 feet. Anything within that bubble is slowed with no save. Projectiles and spell attacks passing through this bubble to attack something on the other side have disadvantage.

Chronobreak

All spells that take 1-10 minutes to cast have their casting time reduced to 1 action.

Parallel Casting

You can focus on two concentration spells at the same time with no penalty. If the spell requires a bonus action to use it on subsequent turns, you may use both concentration spells as a single bonus action. If you have the feat dual-focused, you can concentrate on a third spell, incurring the same penalities on the third spell as the feat specifies.

Quicken

While under the effects of Haste, you can cast a second spell in lieu of making an additional attack. You are able to cast two non-cantrip spells in this manner.

Look at the Time

You take two turns on the first turn of combat and every three turns afterwards (1, 4, 7, etc).

Time Lord

Your Time Stop now lasts a minimum of 3 rounds. Secondly, when casting Time Stop, you may selectively designate which creatures and which areas are affected by Time Stop. Any spells or attacks cast into the time stopped areas hang frozen in time until the Time Stop ends, at which point they resume their normal trajectory. Lastly, you may choose to extend the duration of Time Stop by using your concentration and spending your action, movement action, and bonus action each turn. If you are moved out of range of Time Stop or if you lose concentration, Time Stop ends. The difficulty of maintaining concentration on this spell increases by 1 per turn you spend this way.

Rewind Time

Once per day, as a reaction, you may rewind yourself 3 turns back. You regain any lost health, spell slots, abilities, item charges, expended items on your person, and any conditions you have incurred in the past 3 turns are undone. You and all items you are carrying (but not creatures) are returned to a state they were in 3 turns prior.

Smart Gun

You gain proficiency in firearms. Furthermore, any firearm you are attuned to is now considered a smart gun, allowing you to use your intelligence in place of your dexterity for attack and damage rolls. You can attune to any firearm, even if such a firearm would normally not allow for attunement.

Tinkerer

The casting time of Fabricate is reduced from 10 minutes to 1 action. Additionally, you gain proficiency in Tinker Tools, along with the "Magical Tinkering" trait.

Magical Tinkering: You learn how to invest a spark of magic into mundane objects. To use this ability, you must have tinker's tools in hand. You then touch a Tiny nonmagical object as an action and give it one of the following magical properties of your choice:

  • The object sheds bright light in a 5-foot radius and dim light for an additional 5 feet.
  • Whenever tapped by a creature, the object emits a recorded message that can be heard up to 10 feet away. You utter the message when you bestow this property on the object, and the recording can be no more than 6 seconds long.
  • The object continuously emits your choice of an odor or a nonverbal sound (wind, waves, chirping, or the like). The chosen phenomenon is perceivable up to 10 feet away.
  • A static visual effect appears on one of the object's surfaces. This effect can be a picture, up to 25 words of text, lines and shapes, or a mixture of these elements, as you like.

The chosen property lasts indefinitely. As an action, you can touch the object and end the property early. You can bestow magic on multiple objects, touching one object each time you use this feature, though a single object can only bear one property at a time. The maximum number of objects you can affect with this feature at one time is equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum of one object). If you try to exceed your maximum, the oldest property immediately ends, and then the new property applies.

Magical Efficiency

The number of uses for all items that are attuned to you increases by 1. Additionally, if the item requires a dice roll to restore expended charges, maximize that roll.

Magical Strike

Whenever you cast a spell or cantrip, you may shoot a firearm as a bonus action.

Cram Session

Every morning while preparing spells, you may choose to temporarily learn three artificer infusions that you qualify for. You lose knowledge of these three infusions upon long rest.

Spell Storage

0 times per day, you can imbue any item with a spell you can cast level 5th and below. Any creature can cast the spell stored in this item as if it were a spell scroll. You must expend the spell slot to store the specified spell inside of an item and the item loses whatever spell it is storing upon long rest.

Railgun (Firearm)

Magical firearm. Requires attunement. Range 200/600. This magical firearm is powered by the ambient energy of your magical spells. The railgun has 10 power levels. Starting at power level 0, every spell you cast charges the power level of your railgun by an amount equal to the level of the spell you cast. Cantrips and spells cast using Signature Spell are so efficient, they do not provide any ambient charge for the Railgun. Firing a shot resets the charge level to 0. Whenver 1 minute elapses since you fired your last shot, the charge level decays by 1 per round until it reaches 0.

The Railgun is a ranged piercing firearm that deals xd12+x damage, where x is the charge level, and it has a critical strike multiplier of x3. At maximum charge (level 10), the railgun pierces through all targets in a line. The railgun is so complex, it is only usable by those with the talent to utilize it.

Magic Item Mastery

Your maximum attunement slots increases by 3. You can now attune/deattune to any number of magic items while taking a short or long rest. Additionally, you also ignore all class, race, spell, and level requirements when attuning to a magic item.

Antimagic Hardening

All magical items you wield are immune to the effects of antimagic field.

Savant

You gain the following traits/spells:
  • Magic Shield: You gain a +1 bonus to all saving throws for each magic item you are attuned to.
  • Target Dummmy: As a reaction when you are hit by an attack, you can drop one of your magical attunements to create a fake version of yourself that takes the hit while you teleport away a distance equal to your speed.
  • Contingent Attunement: You gain a new type of infusion. For one attunement slot, you can prepare another spell contingency, as the spell Contingency.
  • Body Augmentation: You gain a new type of infusion. For one attunement slot, you increase any core attribute (str/dex/etc) by +4, up to the limit of 24.
  • Shrink Item: (7th level transmutation spell, casting time 1 action, range: touch). Shrink an item to 1/16th of its size. This lasts for 1 day if cast at 7th level, 7 days if cast at 8th level, and 30 days if cast at 9th level. While an item is shrunk, it can be unshrunk and reshrunk at any time using a command word of your choosing. Does not work on intelligent magic items or magical items with extra dimensional space.
  • Sphere of Annihilation (Conjuration): Cantrip. Requires concentration. You have a Sphere of Anniliation under your control that you can summon as a bonus action. While concentrating on it, you can move this sphere up to 30 feet as a bonus action. Any creature within the path of this sphere must make a dexterity saving throw against your spell save DC or take xd10 force damage (increases based on cantrip damage). A single creature may only take damage from this cantrip once per bonus action. Any non-artifact object is obliterated by this sphere and a creature who dies to this is wholly absorbed by it, as the spell disintegrate.
